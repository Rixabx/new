Harivansh Rai Bachchan

Harivansh Rai Bachchan was an Indian poet of the Nayi Kavita literary movement of early 20th century Hindi literature. He was also a poet of the Hindi Kavi Sammelan. He is best known for his early work Madhushala.

Bachchan was born in Babupatti village of United Provinces of Agra and Oudh, British Raj on 27 November 1907.[5][6] From 1941 to 1957, he taught in the English Department at the Allahabad University and after that, he spent the next two years at St Catharine's College, Cambridge, Cambridge University completing a PhD on W.B. Yeats.[3] He began using the pen name "Bachchan" (meaning child) instead of Shrivastava when he wrote Hindi poetry.

Bachchan was fluent in several Hindi languages (Hindustani, Awadhi). He incorporated a broadly Hindustani vocabulary,[8] written in Devanagari script.[7] While he could not read Persian script,[7] he was influenced by Persian and Urdu poetry, particularly Omar Khayyam.

Bachchan's work has been used in movies and music. For example, couplets of his work "Agneepath" are used throughout the 1990 movie Agneepath, starring Amitabh Bachchan, and later in the 2012 remake Agneepath, starring Hrithik Roshan.
